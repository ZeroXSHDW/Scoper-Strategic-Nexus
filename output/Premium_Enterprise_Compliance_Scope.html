Regulatory Framework Analysis

PART I.b: COMPLIANCE

1.5 Regulatory Alignment: Global Regulated Enterprise (DORA, FRB, HKMA, PRA)

Narrative: A unified global compliance scoping document consolidating DORA, FRB, HKMA, and PRA requirements.

FRAMEWORK DORA

ICT Asset Criticality Mapping

Under DORA, scoping must start with the ICT Asset Register. Every asset in scope must be mapped to a 'Critical or Important Function' (CIF). If an asset supports a CIF, it is subject to more rigorous testing and reporting requirements.

Third-Party ICT Risk

DORA requires that testing includes third-party ICT service providers where they support critical or important functions. This scoping pack must include a list of involved third parties and confirmation of their authorization to test.

Threat Intelligence Requirements

For TLPT (Threat-Led Penetration Testing), the scope must be driven by current, relevant threat intelligence. The scenarios defined in this pack should match the TTPs of actors targeting the financial sector.

FRAMEWORK FRB

SR 13-1 / Vendor Risk

The scoping must encompass critical third-party vendors and external facing infrastructure in alignment with FRB supervision expectations.

FRAMEWORK HKMA

iCAST Execution

Simulations must cover the 'Assume Breach' methodology to assess the detection and response capabilities of the institution's Security Operations Center (SOC).

FRAMEWORK PRA

CBEST Intelligence-Led Testing

Testing must be driven by bespoke threat intelligence targeting the specific critical business services (CBS) of the UK financial institution.

FRAMEWORK MITRE

T1190 - Exploit Public-Facing Application

Adversaries may attempt to take advantage of a weakness in an Internet-facing computer or program using software, data, or commands in order to cause unintended or unanticipated behavior.

T1078 - Valid Accounts

Adversaries may obtain and abuse credentials of existing accounts as a means of gaining Initial Access, Persistence, Privilege Escalation, or Defense Evasion.

T1566 - Phishing

Adversaries may send phishing messages to gain access to victim systems. All forms of phishing are electronically delivered social engineering.

T1059 - Command and Scripting Interpreter

Adversaries may abuse command and script interpreters to execute commands, scripts, or binaries.

FRAMEWORK OWASP

A01:2021-Broken Access Control

Testing must validate that users cannot act outside of their intended permissions. Failures typically lead to unauthorized information disclosure, modification, or destruction of all data.

A02:2021-Cryptographic Failures

Testing must verify the protection of sensitive data in transit and at rest. This includes passwords, credit card numbers, health records, personal information, and business secrets.

A03:2021-Injection

Testing must validate that user-supplied data is not interpreted as commands or queries (e.g., SQL, NoSQL, OS command, ORM, LDAP, and EL expression or OGNL injection).

A04:2021-Insecure Design

Testing must evaluate the architectural design for missing or ineffective control design. Insecure design cannot be fixed by a perfect implementation.

FRAMEWORK CIS

Control 1: Inventory and Control of Enterprise Assets

Actively manage (inventory, track, and correct) all enterprise assets connected to the infrastructure physically, virtually, remotely, and those within cloud environments.

Control 4: Secure Configuration of Enterprise Assets and Software

Establish and maintain the secure configuration of enterprise assets and software.

Control 12: Network Infrastructure Management

Establish, implement, and actively manage network devices, in order to prevent attackers from exploiting vulnerable network services and access points.

PART III: EXECUTION
PT-NET-EXT External Network Penetration Test

A structured assessment of internet-facing infrastructure including firewalls, VPNs, mail gateways, and publicly reachable hosts. Testers simulate real-world attackers attempting to gain unauthorised access from outside the perimeter. Findings are rated by exploitability and business impact.

2 Days | €3,500 Budgetary ALLOC
PT-APP-WEB Web Application Penetration Test

Manual-led security testing of web applications aligned to the OWASP Top 10 and WSTG, covering injection flaws, broken authentication, sensitive data exposure, XML external entities, broken access control, security misconfigurations, XSS, insecure deserialisation, and business logic vulnerabilities.

3 Days | €4,000 Budgetary ALLOC
PT-APP-API REST/GraphQL API Security Testing

Targeted assessment of REST, SOAP, and GraphQL APIs using the OWASP API Security Top 10 framework. Covers broken object-level authorisation, excessive data exposure, lack of rate limiting, broken function-level authorisation, mass assignment, security misconfiguration, and injection flaws.

2 Days | €3,500 Budgetary ALLOC
RT-FULL Full Red Team Engagement

An adversary simulation engagement where a dedicated red team uses realistic threat actor TTPs to attack the organisation across all vectors — digital, physical, and human — without prior disclosure to defenders. Measures the organisation's detection, response, and containment capabilities.

17 Days | €25,000 Budgetary ALLOC
PT-CLD-INF Cloud Infrastructure Penetration Test

Assessment of cloud-native infrastructure, IAM, and resource configurations.

3 Days | €5,000 Budgetary ALLOC

Testing must be immediately paused if any of the following occur:

  • [ ] System becomes unresponsive or crashes
  • [ ] Business-critical service disruption detected
  • [ ] Alert from security operations center
  • [ ] Unexpected data access (beyond test scope)
  • [ ] Any legal concern raised
  • [ ] Client requests immediate halt
  • [ ] Evidence of active compromise by third party

5.2 Kill Switch Procedure

STEP 1: Testing engineer identifies kill switch trigger STEP 2: Immediately cease ALL active testing STEP 3: Document: - What triggered the stop - Last command/action performed - Systems affected - Evidence collected STEP 4: Contact Client Emergency Contact within 15 minutes STEP 5: Await written authorization before resuming STEP 6: Resume only after root cause addressed

None No observable impact Continue testing
