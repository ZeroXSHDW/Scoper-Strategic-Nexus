Regulatory Framework Analysis

PART I.b: COMPLIANCE

1.5 Regulatory Alignment: Global Regulated Enterprise (DORA, FRB, HKMA, PRA)

Narrative: A unified global compliance scoping document consolidating DORA, FRB, HKMA, and PRA requirements.

FRAMEWORK DORA

Digital Operational Resilience Act (EU 2022/2554)

DORA enforces a unified, pan-European approach to operational resilience. Financial entities must demonstrate the capability to withstand, respond to, and recover from all types of ICT-related disruptions and threats.

ICT Asset Criticality & Risk Management

Scoping must strictly map to the ICT Asset Register. Every in-scope asset is evaluated against 'Critical or Important Functions' (CIFs). Assets supporting CIFs require rigorous continuous testing, monitoring, and Board-level reporting.

Third-Party ICT Risk Oversight

Testing explicitly encompasses critical third-party ICT service providers (CTPPs). Financial entities are accountable for the security posture of their entire supply chain, mandating authorization and joint-testing protocols.

Threat-Led Penetration Testing (TLPT)

Advanced TLPT engagements are required to mirror the TTPs of advanced persistent threats (APTs) targeting the EU financial sector. Scenarios must be bespoke, intelligence-driven, and validated by the relevant competent authorities.

FRAMEWORK FRB

SR 13-1 / Third-Party Risk Management

Strict scoping inclusion of critical third-party vendors and external-facing infrastructure, aligning with the Federal Reserve Board's expectations for comprehensive vendor risk oversight.

SR 14-8 / Consolidated Recovery Planning

Validation that the organization's technical controls support its consolidated recovery plans, ensuring the ability to resume core business lines under severe cyber distress.

Sound Practices for Cyber Resilience

Evaluation of the institution's alignment with interagency guidelines on cyber resilience, focusing on governance, dependency management, and incident response readiness.

FRAMEWORK HKMA

Cybersecurity Fortification Initiative (CFI) 2.0

Comprehensive alignment with the HKMA's updated CFI 2.0 requirements, ensuring the Authorized Institution (AI) maintains robust cyber resilience.

iCAST Execution & 'Assume Breach'

Simulations must encompass the Intelligence-led Cyber Attack Simulation Testing (iCAST) methodology, utilizing 'Assume Breach' paradigms to stress-test the SOC's detection, response, and containment capabilities.

Cyber Resilience Assessment Framework (C-RAF)

Scoping supports the C-RAF inherent risk assessment, calibrating the depth of technical validation based on the institution's designated risk profile (Low, Medium, or High).

FRAMEWORK PRA

CBEST Intelligence-Led Testing Framework

Testing must be driven by bespoke, high-fidelity threat intelligence targeting the Critical Business Services (CBS) of UK financial institutions, complying with PRA and FCA joint guidelines.

STAR-FS & Supervisory Statements

Alignment with SS1/21 (Operational resilience: Impact tolerances) to ensure the institution can remain within its impact tolerances during severe but plausible cyber attack scenarios.

Systemic Risk & Contagion Assessment

Simulations must model the potential for systemic contagion affecting the wider UK financial system, validating segregation controls and recovery playbooks.

FRAMEWORK MITRE

Initial Access & Execution Strategies

Scenarios evaluate adversarial techniques such as Spearphishing (T1566), Valid Accounts (T1078), and External Remote Services (T1133) to breach the perimeter and establish initial footholds.

Defense Evasion & Persistence

Testing validates the SOC's ability to detect advanced evasion techniques, including Process Injection (T1055), Obfuscated Files/Information (T1027), and Scheduled Task/Job manipulation (T1053).

Credential Access & Lateral Movement

Assessments simulate attempts to dump credentials (T1003) and move laterally via SMB/Windows Admin Shares (T1021.002) and Pass the Hash (T1550.002), challenging internal zero-trust boundaries.

Command and Control (C2) & Exfiltration

Validation of egress controls against Application Layer Protocol (T1071) C2 channels and automated Data Exfiltration (T1048), ensuring sensitive client data remains contained.

FRAMEWORK OWASP

A01:2021 - Broken Access Control

Rigorous testing to ensure cryptographic boundaries and authorization schemas prevent horizontal and vertical privilege escalation, unauthorized data access, and API manipulation.

A02:2021 - Cryptographic Failures

Verification of data-at-rest and data-in-transit encryption standards, evaluating TLS configurations, key management lifecycle, and protection against cryptographic downgrades.

A03:2021 - Injection

Validation against payload injection vulnerabilities, including SQL, NoSQL, ORM, and OS Command injections, ensuring input sanitization and parameterized query enforcement.

A04:2021 - Insecure Design & Architecture

Threat modeling and architectural review to identify inherent design flaws that cannot be mitigated through patching, emphasizing secure-by-design principles early in the SDLC.

FRAMEWORK CIS

Implementation Group 3 (IG3) Baselines

Scoping aligns with CIS IG3, designed for mature organizations targeted by sophisticated adversaries, demanding the highest level of comprehensive cyber defense implementation.

Control 1 & 2: Enterprise Asset and Software Inventory

Active management, discovery, and tracking of all connected enterprise assets and software to eliminate shadow IT and establish a definitive attack surface baseline.

Control 4: Secure Configuration

Establishment and continuous enforcement of hardened secure configurations across enterprise assets, cloud infrastructure, and software applications.

Technical Scope of Work

PART III: EXECUTION
PT-NET-EXT External Network Penetration Test

A structured assessment of internet-facing infrastructure including firewalls, VPNs, mail gateways, and publicly reachable hosts. Testers simulate real-world attackers attempting to gain unauthorised access from outside the perimeter. Findings are rated by exploitability and business impact.

Expected Duration: 2 Days
PT-APP-WEB Web Application Penetration Test

Manual-led security testing of web applications aligned to the OWASP Top 10 and WSTG, covering injection flaws, broken authentication, sensitive data exposure, XML external entities, broken access control, security misconfigurations, XSS, insecure deserialisation, and business logic vulnerabilities.

Expected Duration: 3 Days
PT-APP-API REST/GraphQL API Security Testing

Targeted assessment of REST, SOAP, and GraphQL APIs using the OWASP API Security Top 10 framework. Covers broken object-level authorisation, excessive data exposure, lack of rate limiting, broken function-level authorisation, mass assignment, security misconfiguration, and injection flaws.

Expected Duration: 2 Days
RT-FULL Full Red Team Engagement

An adversary simulation engagement where a dedicated red team uses realistic threat actor TTPs to attack the organisation across all vectors — digital, physical, and human — without prior disclosure to defenders. Measures the organisation's detection, response, and containment capabilities.

Expected Duration: 17 Days
PT-CLD-INF Cloud Infrastructure Penetration Test

Assessment of cloud-native infrastructure, IAM, and resource configurations.

Expected Duration: 3 Days

5. Safety Protocols

PART VIII: RULES OF ENGAGEMENT

5.1 Kill Switch Criteria

Testing must be immediately paused if any of the following occur:

  • [ ] System becomes unresponsive or crashes
  • [ ] Business-critical service disruption detected
  • [ ] Alert from security operations center
  • [ ] Unexpected data access (beyond test scope)
  • [ ] Any legal concern raised
  • [ ] Client requests immediate halt
  • [ ] Evidence of active compromise by third party

5.2 Kill Switch Procedure

STEP 1: Testing engineer identifies kill switch trigger STEP 2: Immediately cease ALL active testing STEP 3: Document: - What triggered the stop - Last command/action performed - Systems affected - Evidence collected STEP 4: Contact Client Emergency Contact within 15 minutes STEP 5: Await written authorization before resuming STEP 6: Resume only after root cause addressed

5.3 System Impact Thresholds

Impact Level Definition Required Action
None No observable impact Continue testing
Minor Brief slowdown, normal logs Document, continue
Moderate Service degradation Pause, notify client
Severe Service unavailable STOP, emergency protocol
Critical Data breach/financial impact STOP, full incident response

``` EVIDENCE COLLECTION RULES:

  2. Screenshots preferred over raw data

  4. Hash and preserve original evidence

  6. Delete evidence within 90 days of engagement close
  7. Evidence accessible only to named team members ```

6.3 Data Exfiltration Prevention

  • [ ] Proof-of-concept only (no actual data theft)
  • [ ] If sensitive data encountered, immediately document and stop
  • [ ] Any exfiltrated data will be securely destroyed
  • [ ] Client notified immediately if sensitive data accessed
